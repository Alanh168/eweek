Dean’s Welcome



Welcome to the celebration of Engineers Week (EWeek) 2016 at the University of Miami College of Engineering! EWeek is a national program that shows the world how the engineering profession makes a world of difference, and also is an opportunity to increase awareness and public dialogue about the critical need for engineers in our society.

“Engineers make a world of difference. From new farming equipment and safer drinking water to electric cars and faster microchips, engineers use their knowledge to improve people’s lives in meaningful ways. Engineers are creative problem solvers. They have a vision for how something should work and are dedicated to making it better, faster, or more efficient. Engineers help shape the future. They use the latest science, tools, and technology to bring ideas to life. Engineering is essential to our health, happiness, and safety. From the grandest skyscrapers to microscopic medical devices, it is impossible to imagine life without engineering.”

The College of Engineering of the University of Miami fully endorses the the goals set forth by President Julio Frenk at his recent inauguration. Engineers will help create a University that is excellent, relevant, hemispheric and exemplary.

Through programs such as the Society of Hispanic Engineers Entrepreneurship Forum, the Society of Women Engineers Introduce a Girl to Engineering Day, the National Society of Black Engineers Professional Forum on Diversity and Stem and indeed through each and every activity this week, we will strive to demonstrate these values. On behalf of the students, faculty and staff, I invite you to join us for EWeek 2016!
